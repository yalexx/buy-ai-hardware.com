What Makes Good AI Hardware?

When you buy AI hardware, the key metrics are: AI inference throughput (measured in TOPS — Tera Operations Per Second), memory bandwidth, power consumption, and software ecosystem. A device with 67 TOPS like the NVIDIA Jetson Orin Nano can run Llama 3 8B at 15 tokens per second — fast enough for fluid conversations and real-time automation tasks.

Memory matters too. 8GB of unified LPDDR5 RAM means the CPU, GPU, and AI accelerators all share fast memory. This is fundamentally different from a PC where the GPU has its own separate VRAM. Unified memory is more efficient for AI inference workloads, which is why Jetson Orin Nano punches above its weight against much larger desktop GPUs.
